Hacking Fedora 4

We can rebuild it. We have the technology. Better…stronger…faster.

This week at code4lib, we’re hosting a Developer Challenge to get hacking on and around Fedora 4.

Everything we have so far, you’ll find on Github: https://github.com/futures

We very much believe that “The coolest thing to do with your data will be thought of by someone else”, but here are some ideas for where to start:

  • Indexing service
  • Sequencers
    • Amazon Glacier Storage Service

We’ll be awarding prizes for the Developer Challenge this Thursday, which include a $500.00 first prize, a $250.00 second prize plus bottles from our prize list).

The APIs: TBA. Currently, we have the legacy Fedora 3.x APIs implemented and a subset of the CDL Storage Service APIs stubbed out.

The Architecture: TBA.

The mailing list: ff-tech

The wiki: https://wiki.duraspace.org/display/FF/

Lies, Damn Lies and Benchmarks: TBA, some https://wiki.duraspace.org/display/FF/Fedora+3.6.2+test+results and visualizations have been collected for Fedora 3.6.2.

One of the things we’re working on is integrating asynchronous storage with Fedora, namely Amazon Glacier. We’re definitely looking for feedback in this area (e.g. what should the APIs for dealing with asynchronous storage look like?) and expect there’s a lot of opportunity here for some good hacking.
